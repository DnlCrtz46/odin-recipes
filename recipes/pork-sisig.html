Pork Sisig

Description

Pork sisig is a delectable Filipino dish renowned for its bold and savory flavors. It features finely chopped pork, often including pig's face and ears, marinated and then pan-fried until crispy. The dish is then mixed with aromatic ingredients like onions, garlic, and chili peppers, creating a harmonious blend of textures and tastes. Served on a sizzling hotplate, pork sisig is often topped with a raw egg that cooks upon contact, adding a luscious richness to the dish. With its satisfying crunch and enticing combination of flavors, pork sisig is a beloved culinary delight that captures the essence of Filipino cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound pork (pig's face, ears, belly, or a combination), boiled and grilled, then finely chopped
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 to 3 pieces red or green chili peppers (siling labuyo), minced
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon calamansi or lemon juice
  • 1 raw egg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional garnishes: chopped green onions, chopped fresh cilantro, crispy pork skin (chicharrón), chopped red onions

Steps

  1. Boil the pork until tender. Once cooked, grill or broil it to achieve a crispy texture. Finely chop the grilled pork into small pieces.
  2. In a pan, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Sauté the minced garlic until fragrant and slightly golden. Add the chopped onions and cook until they become translucent.
  3. Add the finely chopped grilled pork to the pan. Allow it to cook and get slightly crispy.
  4. Mix in the minced chili peppers. Adjust the quantity according to your desired level of spiciness.
  5. Pour in the soy sauce and stir to evenly distribute the flavors.
  6. Add the mayonnaise and calamansi or lemon juice to the pan. Mix well to coat the pork and create a creamy texture.
  7. Taste the mixture and season with salt and pepper as needed. The soy sauce and mayonnaise contribute to the overall seasoning.
  8. Transfer the pork sisig mixture to a sizzling hotplate. You can also use a regular serving dish if a hotplate is not available.
  9. Crack a raw egg on top of the hot sizzling sisig. The heat of the hotplate will cook the egg as it sizzles.
  10. Garnish the pork sisig with chopped green onions, fresh cilantro, crispy pork skin (chicharrón), or chopped red onions for added flavor and texture.
