Pork Sinigang

Description

Pork sinigang is a popular Filipino sour soup known for its tangy and savory flavors. This comforting dish features tender pork chunks simmered with an assortment of vegetables, such as kangkong (water spinach), radish, eggplant, and string beans, in a sour broth made from tamarind or other souring agents. The combination of sourness from the broth and the richness of the pork creates a harmonious balance that's both refreshing and hearty. Often served over steamed rice, pork sinigang showcases the vibrant and diverse flavors that define Filipino cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound pork (pork ribs, pork belly, or pork shoulder), cut into chunks
  • 1 medium onion, peeled and sliced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 medium radish, peeled and sliced
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 cup string beans (green beans), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach), leaves and tender stems
  • 2 to 3 pieces green chili peppers (siling pansigang), optional
  • 1 to 2 tablespoons fish sauce (patis)
  • Tamarind or tamarind mix (sinigang mix) for the sour flavor
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Water
  • Cooking oil

Steps

  1. In a large pot, heat a bit of cooking oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and sauté until they become translucent. Add the sliced tomatoes and cook until they start to soften and release their juices.
  2. Add the pork chunks to the pot and sauté for a few minutes until they are lightly browned. Pour enough water into the pot to cover the pork. Bring it to a boil and then let it simmer until the pork starts to become tender. Skim off any impurities that rise to the surface.
  3. Add the sliced radish to the pot and continue simmering until the radish is slightly softened. Add the sliced eggplant and string beans, and let them cook until all the vegetables are tender.
  4. Add fish sauce (patis) to the pot for a savory flavor. Adjust the amount according to your taste preference. Add tamarind or sinigang mix to achieve the desired sourness. Start with a small amount and gradually add more until you reach your preferred level of sourness.
  5. Add the kangkong (water spinach) leaves and tender stems to the pot. Stir and let them wilt in the broth.
  6. Taste the broth and adjust the seasoning by adding salt, pepper, or more fish sauce if needed. If desired, add the green chili peppers for a bit of heat.
  7. Serve the pork sinigang hot over steamed rice. The flavorful and tangy broth complements the tender pork and variety of vegetables.
